About the Program

The Sexual Assault Prevention Program (SAPR) provides training for the force, advice to leaders, advocacy for victims of sexual assault, and management of the SAPR professional force.

SAPR also provides connection to community resources for victims of domestic violence and limited services for individuals experiencing sexual harassment.

Who We Serve

PA NG SAPR can help all members of the military, their adult dependents, federalized civilians (DoD/DA/Contractors), and victims of sexual assault when there is a military nexus.

What is Sexual Assault?

Any sexual act committed without clear, voluntary, and informed consent.

How to Get Help

Call the PA NG SAPR Hotline: 866-922-6275

Reporting Options

Eligible victims can elect a reporting option (Restricted or Unrestricted) by signing a DD Form 2910. Victims may also elect to file a CATCH entry by working with a SARC to file a DD Form 2910-4.

Retaliation

Retaliation has no place in our ranks. If a victim, SAPR professional, or someone supporting the victim feels they are the target of retaliation, please notify the Inspector General. SAPR can assist with connecting you to the Inspector General.
